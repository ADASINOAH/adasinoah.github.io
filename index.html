About

I hold a BSc in Electrical & Electronic Engineering from Ashesi University (2024), where I was a MasterCard Foundation Scholar. My final-year capstone introduced a YOLOv8-based electronic scarecrow for protecting rice farms from predator birds, combining computer vision with low-cost hardware deterrents.

I currently serve as a Teaching Assistant at Ashesi for graduate and undergraduate courses in Signals & Systems, Communication Systems, and Digital Signal Processing, and as a GRE / GMAT / SAT Mathematics Lecturer at EducationUSA, ACE Consult Ltd. I'm also an AI for Health Research Fellow with the African Leadership Academy, studying treated drinking-water quality in galamsey-affected communities of Ghana.

Outside the lab I led the Galaxy Space Campus Network at Ashesi as president, facilitated workshops and STEM outreach, and founded NPath Foundation, a non-profit supporting underprivileged students. I am preparing to begin doctoral studies and welcome collaborations with research groups working on applied signal processing for trustworthy ML and next-generation wireless systems.
